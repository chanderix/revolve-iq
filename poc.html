The POC

What We're Proposing to Prove

A focused 90-day Proof of Concept that demonstrates the full framework on a live portfolio segment — not a simulation. Real data. Real clocks. One completed revolver cycle measured end-to-end.

POC Scope

What's In. What We Need.

POC In Scope
  • Live portfolio segment — 1 active revolver cycle, ~500–2,000 customer accounts
  • All 8 clocks instrumented with daily KPI capture from existing source systems
  • Coverage model live — daily ratio update + Day 90 projection refreshed automatically
  • Escalation protocol active — Day 60 and Day 75 checkpoints executed per playbook
  • PJM Clock 8 included — UB model remittance tracking for PJM footprint accounts
What We Need From You
  • System access (read-only) — CIS, ETRM, EDI gateway, AR, PJM eTools export
  • Data extract schedule — daily automated exports or API connections from each system
  • Cross-functional participation — Treasury, Billing Ops, AR, EDI, Energy Settlements
  • Executive sponsor — CFO or VP Treasury to chair the Day 60 and 75 checkpoints
  • Weekly governance cadence — Tuesday standing meeting per playbook agenda
Out of scope for POC:  New system builds  ·  ERP integration  ·  Production infrastructure  ·  Customer-facing changes

Success Criteria

How We Define POC Success

We will declare the POC successful if we can answer YES to all of the following by Week 12:

01 / 06

Did we see risk before Day 75?

At least one clock triggered an amber or red alert that was acted upon before the repayment risk window opened.

02 / 06

Was coverage ratio monitored daily?

The coverage ratio was updated and distributed every business day without manual intervention.

03 / 06

Did escalations happen on time?

Day 60 and Day 75 checkpoints executed per playbook, with documented owner responses within SLA.

04 / 06

Was unbilled balance controlled?

Unbilled balance remained below 8% of cycle revenue, with any breach resolved within 3 business days.

05 / 06

Was PJM settled without surprise?

ISO charges accrued within 5% of actuals at final statement, with no unbudgeted true-up over $200K.

06 / 06

Was the revolver repaid on time?

Coverage ratio reached 1.0× by Day 87 and the full draw balance was repaid on or before Day 90/91.
